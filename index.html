What is our mission

CodePhil aims to teach, empower, and connect the next generation in Northern Samar, Philippines through programming. Our initiatives will allow low-income high school students to learn the valuable and marketable skills of coding, generate interests in technology careers, and create technological solutions for community-building.

Who are our partners

CodePhil is funded by Columbia University, MIT's Society of Women Engineers, and the United States Department of State. Our partners include Bagong Kulturang Pinoy, a non-profit organization whose mission is to promote literacy among children in poverty-stricken areas of the Philippines and the Local Government Unit of Lavezares, Philippines.

Why we care

We believe that equal access to quality education is the key to a world without poverty. Northern Samar has a poverty incidence rate of 43.5%, one of the highest in the Philippines, and is the 9th poorest province out of 81. To overcome the poverty situation, the Philippines Department of Education (DepEd) recently extended the length of public education by adding two more years to high school. The idea behind "senior high school" is to make students more employable immediately after graduating from high school. Furthermore, DepEd launched a nationwide computerization project for all schools in order to increase the digital literacy of students. By leveraging these assets, CodePhil hopes to build tech skills in high students and help them create technology infrastructures for their community.
